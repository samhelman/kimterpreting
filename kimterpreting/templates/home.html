I am a registered Registered Sign Language Interpreter based in Wokingham, Berkshire. I provide interpretation services between British Sign Language (BSL) and English (Spoken or Written). My interest in British Sign Language began after attending a basic BSL course in 2009. Since then I have worked my way through the Signature NVQ levels in British Sign Language. Whilst working as a Trainee Interpreter, I achieved the SLI Advanced Diploma in BSL-English Interpreting and Translation in 2017 and registered with NRCPD as a Registered Sign Language Interpreter (RSLI). In addition to the formal qualifications, I have completed workshops and courses including Sign Language Linguistics taught by Rachel Sutton-Spence, Theatre Sign's Introduction to Theatre Interpreting and Voices-Off Weekends run by Signamic. I enjoy volunteering and have been fortunate enough to work with organisations such as Sense and at national events including Deaf World, Reading Festival and London Pride.

I have a BSc (hons) degree in Applied Psychology and Sociology from the University of Surrey and have worked in non-interpreting roles in Mental Health and Adult Social Care settings since 2004.

My other interests include amateur dramatics and singing, tending to my garden with the 'assistance' of my cats and cycling. In fact I am so well known for riding my bike that my sign name in the Deaf community is the sign for 'cycling'.

Punctuality and preparation are important to me and I am professional and flexible in my approach.

Please do not hesitate to contact me if you would like to ask any questions. I'm always happy to discuss interpreting and if I am not able to assist, I am part of a wide network of professional interpreters so I will usually be able to put you in touch with someone who can meet your requirements.
